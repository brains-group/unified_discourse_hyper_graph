ARTICLE 1 - KEY DEFINITIONS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
1.1 "Index Account" - An account where interest credits are based 
    on the performance of a stock market index.

1.2 "Fixed Account" - An account earning a guaranteed fixed 
    interest rate.

1.3 "Cap Rate" - The maximum interest rate that can be credited 
    to an index account in any crediting period.

1.4 "Floor Rate" - The minimum interest rate (0%) that will be 
    credited, protecting against market losses.

1.5 "Participation Rate" - The percentage of index gain that is 
    credited to your account.

1.6 "Point-to-Point Method" - Interest crediting based on index 
    change from beginning to end of crediting period (annual).

ARTICLE 3 - HOW INDEX CREDITING WORKS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
3.1 CREDITING CALCULATION:
    On each policy anniversary, we calculate:
    
    Index % Change = (Ending Index Value - Starting Index Value) 
                     ÷ Starting Index Value
    
    Interest Credited = Index % Change × Participation Rate
                       (subject to Cap and Floor)

3.2 EXAMPLE:
    Assume S&P 500 Index:
    • Started at 4,000
    • Ended at 4,600
    • Gain: 15%
    • With 100% participation and 10.5% cap
    • Interest credited: 10.5% (capped)
    
    If index declined 5%:
    • Floor protects at 0%
    • Interest credited: 0% (no loss)

3.3 CRITICAL: You never lose money due to market decline. The 
    worst case is 0% interest for that year.
